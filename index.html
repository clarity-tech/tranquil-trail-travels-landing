Chapter Four

Beyond the Ordinary

Places that exist in the space between legend and reality. Destinations most travelers never reach.

World's Largest River Island

Majuli Island

Drift to Majuli on a ferry across the Brahmaputra. Here, Vaishnavite monasteries called Satras have preserved 500-year-old dance, theatre, and mask-making traditions. Watch artisans shape sacred masks as time moves at the river's pace.

Valley of Flowers of the East

Dzukou Valley

Trek through ancient Naga trails to reach Dzukou — a high-altitude valley that bursts into carpets of lilies and rhododendrons each season. At 2,452 metres, this is where the earth touches the sky.

UNESCO World Heritage Landscape

Ziro Valley

The Apatani people of Ziro have practiced sustainable rice-fish farming for centuries in a landscape so pristine, UNESCO protects it. Walk paddy bunds, meet elders with traditional facial tattoos, and taste rice beer made from ancient recipes.
